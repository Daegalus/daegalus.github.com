Mailing Lists Need to Go

Sep 28th, 2011

One thing that really bugs me is the fact that open source projects or just projects in general are still using mailing lists as their primary form of communciation to the community and their users. The only downside is that with modern day technologies, mailing lists are pretty old news and the general users won’t use them. No one wants their inbox spammed, the Web UIs for most are attrociously simple and messy, and no one is gonna bother going through the archives to find something.

Also, in order to do anything on the mailing list, you have to join it, and send messages, but in the process of joining, you will get so many more emails than you care for.

The time it takes you to setup a mailing list, you can setup a forum, which more people are willing to use. If you care about getting emails, most popular forums have subscriptions that will email you whenever someone replies, also email you digests and other things.

I just don’t see why people are using draconian methods that limits their scope.

Now, a valid alternative is Google Groups. They have a nice middleground between Forum and Mailing list. It is not perfect, but much easier to use.

But I just think the term Mailing List needs to be stop being used for Google Groups. Many people see that and ignore it because they have the misconception that it is the old style mailing list. Either call it a Google Group, or use a proper forum.

The reason I am ranting about this, because I was sitting in IRC and a primary dev on a project was responding to a complaint of something being removed. He mentioned that he polled the mailing list to see others thoughts. THe problem is, I checked the mailing list, and it had about 40 people, when the software has 100s of people using it. I just thought that was completely stupid to think only poll a small mailing list to dictate the usage of a popular feature.

Btw, I will add, that I believe an IRC channel is more popular than a mailing lists. But if you are absolutely adamant about using a mailing list, please use Google Groups at the very least and link to it as Google Groups and not “Mailing List”.

I know many won’t agree, but it bugs me, so hence this rant.
